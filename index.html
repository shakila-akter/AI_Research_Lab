About Our Lab

Our history, vision, goals, and the values that drive us

The AI Research Lab was established in 2010 within the Department of Computer and Information Science (CIS) at the University of Technology. What began as a small team of three faculty members and ten graduate students has grown into one of the most recognized AI research centers in the region.

Over 15 years, our lab has published 35+ research papers at top-tier venues including NeurIPS, ICML, CVPR, ACL, and AAAI. We have secured over $2 million in grants from government agencies and industry partners.

Our lab is proudly interdisciplinary, welcoming researchers from computer science, mathematics, engineering, and cognitive science backgrounds.

Key Milestones

  • 2010 — Lab founded with 3 faculty members
  • 2013 — First international paper published
  • 2015 — First government grant ($200K)
  • 2017 — Robotics wing established
  • 2019 — Industry partnership signed
  • 2021 — GPU cluster upgraded (128 GPUs)
  • 2023 — 50+ publications milestone
  • 2025 — Best Paper Award at ICML

🔬 Research Areas

Six core areas of AI and Machine Learning investigation

Machine Learning

We develop algorithms that enable computers to learn from data without being explicitly programmed. Our work covers supervised, unsupervised, reinforcement, and federated learning approaches.

Supervised Learning Reinforcement Learning AutoML Federated Learning
Activity Level90%

Deep Learning

We design and train deep neural networks from classic CNNs and RNNs to modern Transformer architectures, focusing on efficiency, interpretability, and edge device deployment.

CNNs Transformers GANs Model Compression
Activity Level85%

Computer Vision

We teach machines to understand visual information from images and video. Research spans object detection, image segmentation, face recognition, and 3D scene reconstruction.

Object Detection Segmentation 3D Reconstruction Video Analysis
Activity Level80%

Natural Language Processing

NLP enables machines to read, understand, and generate human language. We work on machine translation, sentiment analysis, question answering, and large language models (LLMs).

Machine Translation Sentiment Analysis LLMs Text Summarization
Activity Level75%

Robotics

We build intelligent robots that perceive their environment, plan actions, and interact safely with humans. Key areas include SLAM, motion planning, and human-robot interaction.

SLAM Motion Planning HRI Manipulation
Activity Level65%

Data Science

Data science forms the backbone of all our research. We focus on big data analytics, statistical modeling, data visualization, and scalable pipelines for large-scale datasets.

Big Data Visualization Statistical Modeling Data Pipelines

Research Projects

Active and completed research projects across all AI domains

● Active

MediVision: AI-Powered Medical Image Diagnosis

A deep learning system that assists radiologists in diagnosing diseases from X-ray, MRI, and CT scan images using a custom CNN architecture trained on 500,000+ annotated medical images. Current chest X-ray accuracy: 96.3%.

Currently in clinical trial phase at three partner hospitals; deployment expected 2026.

PyTorch CNN Transfer Learning DICOM Python
Started: Jan 2023 Lead: Dr. Sarah Chen Grant: $350,000
● Active

AgriBot: Smart Agricultural Robot

An autonomous ground robot with computer vision and ML to monitor crop health, detect pests, and automate irrigation using SLAM navigation. Field tests showed a 40% reduction in pesticide use.

Deployed across 5 partner farms; expanding to 20 farms by end of 2025.

ROS SLAM OpenCV Raspberry Pi
Started: Mar 2023 Lead: Prof. Ahmed Hassan Grant: $280,000
Ongoing

BanglaLLM: Large Language Model for Bangla

A large language model trained specifically on Bangla (Bengali) text addressing the critical lack of AI tools for low-resource South Asian languages. Pre-trained on 50GB of Bangla text data.

Fine-tuned for summarization, translation, and question answering tasks.

Transformer HuggingFace BERT NLP
Started: Jun 2024 Lead: Dr. Maria Lopez Grant: $220,000
